Introductions

Hello friends...

Allisa and Gabe were married last June on Anini Beach on the island of Kauai. It was a small ceremony (more on that below). We would have liked to invite you all were that possible. It was however always our intention to have some sort of mainland party/reception for our lovely families and friends. It has taken us longer than we hoped to put that together.

In the meantime, our marriage is bearing fruit (more on that below). So this party is meant as a celebration of our marriage as well as a celebration of our coming tot/sprog/baby. Two birds, one stone. Two people (soon to be three), one family, one soirée. Hopefully that's not too confusing. In any case, we have invited you because we would love to see you and to have you share in this celebration of our economy-size bundle of life changes. Please be a dear and RSVP below so we can plan accordingly.

Some of you don't know both of us well. Let us start with some introductions ...

Gabriel Seth Beymer Finch

Gabriel Seth Finch, all 9lbs. 12 oz. of me, was born to Melissa Ann (Boles) Finch and Mark Steven Finch on April the 19th of 1978 at Sacred Heart Hospital in Eugene, Oregon. I was preceded into this world by a sister (my only sibling), Hilary Nicole (Finch) Hutler, currently of Portland, Oregon. I'm told my nickname as a baby was "Gunter the Grunter".

As long as I can remember I have loved drawing, being outdoors, and taking things apart. I feel very blessed to have gotten to grow up in the southern Willamette Valley and remember spending much of my childhood exploring something or other in dappled light under a canopy of mature trees. I also love reading, and that love began with the national treasure that is Mad Magazine (shout out to Alfred E. Neuman and Sergio Aragones). I sought out old issues on trips to thrift store with my mother. My sister and I both spent a lot of time playing video games on an Atari 65XE home computer sent from Germany by our uncle Matt.

High school was a hard time for me. I largely tried to keep my headphones on and avoid eye-contact. Teachers like Sue Markley and Janelle Youngblood helped keep me on track. One of our high school proctors, Mike Paup was a beacon of hope for me and a caring friend during these times. I found my mothers Yamaha acousitc guitar in the attic and taught myself the bassline for Come As You Are

After high school I studied painting, drawing and photography part-time at community college and worked at a variety of jobs over several years (t-shirt printer, dishwasher, line-cook, forklift operator, delivery driver, wildland firefighter). One of my hobbies during this time was fixing up old Schwinn Sturmey-Archer 3-speed bicycles. A girlfriend taught me how to play Freight Train on guitar, which opened up a whole new world of playing.

In my mid-tweties I entered university full-time at the U. of O. and completed a B.S. in Biology. My degree led immediatelly to a position in Los Angeles studying formation of the bones and cartilage that make up the skull. I dislike car trips and I am the cheapest of cheapskate, so I didn't love L.A. Fortunately I was soon able to transfer to a position back in Portland, Oregon in the Lab of Teresa Nicolson helping with studies of the inner ear's mechanical workings. I continued to work on bicycles and began taking longer bike trips, to the coast, to the cascades, etc. I was also running a lot, and completed a half marathon with my friend Dylan.

I worked as a research technician for eight years. It was during this time that I met Allisa(more on that below). Eventually in 2014, partly due to a bleak outlook for science research funding, I decided to embark on a relatively radical career change into computer programming. I enrolled in a 5-month programming school at the start of 2015. Finding programming employment was difficult at first, but I am now happily employed full-time and finding that I am not half bad at this programming stuff. I'm hoping that, as a career choice, it will afford us the ability to stay in Portland where we have made so many wonderful friends.
